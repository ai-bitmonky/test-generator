Solution:

Question:

A. $a = 1, b = 0$
B. $a = 1, b = -1$ ‚úì (Correct)

Solution:

Solution:

Step 2: Substitute into the limit: $$\lim_{x \to \infty} \left[x - 1 + \frac{2}{x+1} - ax - b\right] = 0$$ $$= \lim_{x \to \infty} \left[(1-a)x + (-1-b) + \frac{2}{x+1}\right] = 0$$
Step 3: For the limit to be zero:
  • Coefficient of $x$ must be 0: $1 - a = 0 \implies a = 1$
  • Constant term must be 0: $-1 - b = 0 \implies b = -1$
Key Concept: Polynomial division and comparison of coefficients

Solution:

  • Thus $\frac{e^{1/x} - 1}{e^{1/x} + 1} \to \frac{\infty}{\infty} = 1$

Step 4: Since $g$ is continuous and $g(0) = 0$, we must have $g(x) = xh(x)$ where $h(x)$ is continuous (polynomial).
Key Concept: One-sided limits must match; requires $g(0) = 0$

Step 3: Rationalize the denominator factor: $$\sqrt{a+x} - \sqrt{a-x} = \frac{(a+x) - (a-x)}{\sqrt{a+x} + \sqrt{a-x}} = \frac{2x}{\sqrt{a+x} + \sqrt{a-x}}$$
